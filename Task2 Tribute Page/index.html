Tribute to M. S. Dhoni

"Success is not what you achieve, it's what you overcome."

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as M. S. Dhoni, is a former Indian international cricketer and captain of the Indian national cricket team. He is considered one of the greatest wicket-keeper batsmen in the history of cricket.

Dhoni is known for his calmness and exceptional leadership skills. He led the Indian cricket team to various victories, including the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, and the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.

Some Interesting Facts About Dhoni

  • M. S. Dhoni was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India.
  • He made his international debut in 2004 against Bangladesh in an ODI match.
  • Dhoni is the only captain in cricket history to win all major ICC trophies (World Cup, World Twenty20, and Champions Trophy).
  • Under Dhoni's captaincy, India achieved the number one ranking in Test cricket for the first time in 2009.
  • He is known for his lightning-quick stumpings and excellent wicket-keeping skills.
  • Dhoni holds several records, including the most international stumpings by a wicket-keeper.
  • He retired from international cricket in August 2020, but his impact on the game and his popularity remain unparalleled.
